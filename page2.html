Shinde Chatri

Shinde Chatri, or the Red Palace, is a historic palace in Pune, Maharashtra. It was originally constructed by Shivaji Maharaj for his wife Saibai. The palace has witnessed significant events in Maratha history. Though the current structure is a reconstruction, it remains a popular tourist attraction

Bhuleshwar Mandir

Bhuleshwar Mandir Fort, located near Pune, India, has a rich history dating back to the 17th century. Originally known as Kondana Fort, it has witnessed several battles, including the famous Battle of Bhuleshwar Mandir in 1670. The fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and houses historical structures like the Kalyan Darwaza and Tanaji Malusare's tomb. It's a popular trekking destination and a symbol of Maratha valor.

People Also Ask About Pune

  1. Which are the best historical places to visit in Pune with family?

    1. Shaniwar Wada: One of the most prominent historical places in Pune, this palace dates back to 1732 and presents a marvellous example of Mughal and Maratha architecture, opulence, and grandeur.

    2. Shivneri Fort: Constructed in the 17th century, this hillfort is known as the birthplace of Maratha warrior king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It still has the statues of young Shivaji and his mother Jijabai that add to its elegance.

    3. Vishrambaug Wada: The opulent residence of Peshwa Bajirao II, this sprawling mansion was built in the year 1807, and is a beautiful example of Peshwa's love for grandeur and brilliance.

    4. Chatushrungi Mata Temple: Constructed during Shivaji's rule, this ancient hilltop temple is dedicated to Goddess Chatushrungi and offers the panoramic views of the verdant landscapes.

    5. Shivaji Bridge: Known as Lloyd Bridge, it dates back to the early 20th century and depicts various architectural styles, arched pillars, and Mughal style floral patterns, all giving it a magnificent     appearance.

  2. Which are the famous historical monuments in Pune?

    1. Aga Khan Palace: Built in 1892, this is amongst the most famous historical monuments in Pune and is related to India's freedom movement.

    2. Shindyanchi Chhatri: Built in honour of the great Marathi warrior Mahadji Shinde, this memorial also has a Lord Shiva temple that charms visitors even today.

    3. Parvati Hill and Temple: Considered to be the oldest heritage structure in Pune, this is dedicated to Goddess Parvati and Devadeshwar.

    4. Lal Mahal: Carrying immense historical significance, this was built in 1634 and is now serving as a museum having several relics and pictures of Shivaji that belong to the 16th century.

    Winter months from October to February, prove to be the best time to visit Pune when the temperatures are pleasant and provide the idyllic conditions and perfect atmosphere for sightseeing and adventures.
